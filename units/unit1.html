Unit 1: Introduction & Mindset

My Learning Journey

Identifying Learning Experiences

In Unit 1, I began my self-reflection and academic assessment journey while taking PACE111. Introducing myself in the initial class discussion allowed me to recount and reflect on my educational path—from earning an A.S. in Information Systems to pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science at UMGC. Sharing my experiences with web development, Python, and my recent foray into C++ highlighted the evolution of my technical skills. It is pretty enlightening and reassuring to reflect on the evolution of my skills over time. Initially, C++ posed significant challenges, and I grappled with a fixed mindset intertwined with imposter syndrome as I felt I was way over my head. However, recognizing these mental barriers was a crucial learning experience. The DAACS assessment further illuminated my academic strengths and areas for growth. While I scored highly in motivation and strategies, it became evident that I need to enhance my metacognitive skills, particularly in planning, monitoring, and evaluating my learning processes.

Application of Learning to Future Endeavors

Understanding the importance of a growth mindset is not just an academic exercise but a vital tool for my future professional endeavors. I hope to one day develop AI software for medicine and human augmentation, which will require persistent learning and adaptability from my end. By improving my metacognitive abilities, I am sure that I will more effectively plan and execute complex projects that I will take on that will enhance the quality of life for medical trauma survivors, especially cancer survivors like myself. The strategies I've committed to—such as better planning study sessions and actively seeking feedback—will directly translate to professional settings where project planning and continual self-improvement are crucial. Embracing a mindset focused on mastery rather than just outcomes will allow me to contribute meaningfully to AI in medicine and biotechnology.

Identifying Key Takeaways

The pivotal takeaway from this unit is the transformative power of adopting a growth mindset. Shifting my perspective has already begun alleviating feelings of imposter syndrome and has reignited my passion for learning challenging subjects like C++. I am now more comfortable with C++ than ever before. I am currently working on a project to use my skills to create a Thanksgiving "Turkey Trot" turkey costume with automated features for my daughter's elementary school 2024 "Turkey Trot" event. The most consequential benefit of Unit 1, of course, was the insights gleaned from the DAACS assessment, which provided valuable insights into my learning habits, emphasizing the need for improved metacognition (thinking about thinking). Recognizing that planning, monitoring, and evaluating are not just academic exercises but essential life skills has been illuminating my growth as I finish my degree. This unit has reinforced my belief that personal and professional development will be ever-ongoing, and I will continue developing my self-awareness, resilience, and willingness to seek help and resources when needed.
